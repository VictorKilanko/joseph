# Course 101 — Identity Quiz & Assessment
## Know Thyself: Self-Assessment

**Instructions:** This is a reflective assessment — not a test with right or wrong answers. Answer each question honestly and completely. The goal is self-knowledge, not a score. Set aside 30–45 minutes in a quiet space.

When this course becomes interactive, your responses will build your personal Identity Profile — the foundation for your FRAME assessment in Course 201.

---

## Section A: Self-Perception (Modules 1–2)

**1.** Complete this sentence honestly: *"Most people see me as ____________, but I actually am ____________."*
What is the gap between those two things? What causes it?

---

**2.** Of the three Identity Thieves — Performance, Perception, and Possessions — which one has the strongest grip on your daily decisions? Give a specific recent example.

---

**3.** Rate how much each of the following shapes your sense of worth on a scale of 1 (not at all) to 5 (completely):

| Source of Worth | Your Rating (1–5) |
|---|---|
| Academic or professional achievement | |
| What specific people think of me | |
| Social media presence or following | |
| Material possessions or lifestyle | |
| My relationships and social status | |

Look at your ratings. What does the pattern tell you about where your identity is currently anchored?

---

**4.** Describe the last time you felt your sense of self-worth genuinely threatened. What caused it? How did you respond? What does that response tell you about where your identity was located in that moment?

---

**5.** If every external marker of your identity were removed — no job title, no income, no relationship status, no social following, no academic credential — who would remain? Write 3–5 sentences describing that person.

## Section D: Pressure (Module 5)

**15.** Which of the four pressure tests hits you hardest right now?
- [ ] Popularity Test — changing who I am to maintain social standing
- [ ] Prosperity Test — bending my values when money or opportunity is at stake
- [ ] Adversity Test — losing myself when things go badly wrong
- [ ] Authority Test — shrinking in the presence of power or confidence

Explain why you chose that one. What specific situation in your life is activating it?

---

**16.** Describe the most significant moment of adversity you've faced. Who were you *before* it happened? Who were you *after*? What did it reveal about the foundation your identity was built on?

---

**17.** Name one person in your life right now who tells you the truth about yourself — even when it's uncomfortable. What has that person told you that you needed to hear? If you can't name someone, what does that absence tell you?

**19.** Write your first draft of an **Identity Statement** using this template:

> *I am [name]. I was made to [purpose/function]. I am driven by [values]. I see the world through the lens of [distinctive perspective]. I am at my best when [context or condition]. I am committed to [direction or mission].*

Don't overthink it. Write a draft. You can revise it.

**20.** What is the single most important thing you are taking away from Course 101? What will you do differently — not eventually, but this week — because of it?

---

## Scoring Key

This assessment does not produce a numerical score. It produces a **Self-Portrait** — a written picture of where you are now.

When you complete Course 201 (Frame), your responses here will inform your FRAME profile. Keep your answers. Return to them in three, six, and twelve months. Identity is not a destination — it is a practice.

**Completion:** You have completed Course 101 when you have answered all 20 questions in full and written your Identity Statement.
