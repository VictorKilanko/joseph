# Joseph — Course Curriculum Overview

These four courses are the intellectual and developmental core of the Joseph platform. They are designed in sequence — each building on the last — and together they walk a young person from self-discovery to world impact.

## Curriculum Philosophy

This curriculum is inspired by three streams of thought:

**1. Purpose and Kingdom thinking** — drawn from the work of Dr. Myles Munroe (theologian and leadership philosopher) on identity, potential, and the power of living on purpose.

**2. Gifts-based self-discovery** — drawn from Rick Warren's purpose-driven framework (adapted and expanded here as FRAME) and the insight that every person is shaped by a unique combination of passion, experience, ability, and personality.

**3. Psychology and human development** — including Big Five personality research (Costa & McCrae), Positive Psychology and character strengths (Seligman & Peterson), Flow theory (Csikszentmihalyi), Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan), and strengths-based development (Gallup CliftonStrengths).

| Letter | Dimension | Core Question |

| **F** | **Fire** | What ignites you? |
| **R** | **Roots** | What formed you? |
| **A** | **Abilities** | What can you do? |
| **M** | **Makeup** | How are you wired? |
| **E** | **Expression** | How do you uniquely show up? |

## A Note on Ancient Sources

Throughout these courses, wisdom is drawn from ancient writers introduced by their real-world roles:
- **Solomon** — Philosopher-king, ancient entrepreneur, and the world's wealthiest ruler
- **David** — Warrior-king and history's most celebrated poet
- **Paul** — Roman-trained legal scholar turned movement builder
- **Joseph (of Egypt)** — Dream interpreter, supply chain strategist, and prime minister
- **Nehemiah** — Urban planner and crisis project manager
- **Daniel** — Government strategist and civil servant under foreign rule
- **Luke** — First-century physician and historian
- **Isaiah** — Political advisor and cultural prophet

Their insights belong to all of humanity. We take them seriously regardless of a reader's spiritual background.
